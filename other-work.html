MINIATURE SET DESIGN: SHORT FILM- EKAANT | 2017




I worked with students from the Film and Television Institute of India- Art Director Neeraj Singh and team to make a miniature set for the short film “Ekaant” directed by Sarthak Bhasin. The storyline of the film required the making of a detailed miniature set of an old city, with dilapidated and destroyed buildings. The inspiration came from a city in Syria that was destroyed due to the ongoing unrest.

A lot of research was done in terms of materials since it was important to make the set as realistic as possible. Scale played a significant role as well. From trying innumerable options with different materials and scales to eventually coming up with the final output, this journey was a very interesting learning experience and an opportunity to develop and demonstrate immense creativity. The film was wrapped up and screened in FTII a few months later, and received extensive adulation for the realistic set and art direction.

The film later travelled to various festivals, and won many nominations and awards, the most notable of which are:

  • The 66th National Film Award for Art Direction- “For it’s surrealistic design and imagery of a bleak impending catastrophe”. Honored by the Vice President of India at Vigyan Bhavan, Delhi.
  • Best Short Fiction Award at the Tbilisi International Student Film Festival, Georgia.
  • Selected for the Beijing Film Academy’s International Student Film & Video Festival, China
